DMX: Career

Beginnings of musical Career

Earl Simmons, aka DMX, was into hip hop music since he was 14 and performing at different venues with local rapper Ready Ron.

Unfortunately, DMX was often sent to jail for his crimes like robbery carjacking and began writing songs whilst he was in prison.

Further, Simmons released his own mixtapes and cover videos and earned few dollars from selling them.

After a while, he was acknowledged by several record labels after listing to his mixtapes and was signed by Columbia Records in 1992.

As a matter of fact, he dropped his first-ever single entitled Born Loser in 1992 in collaboration with Ruffhouse Records.

Later in February 1998, the late rapper DMX launched a single called Get at Me Dog with the Def Jam Recordings.

Studio Albums of DMX

After a while, Earl Simmons, aka DMX, started to focus on releasing a studio album.

As a result, he dropped his first-ever studio album entitled It’s Dark and Hell Is Hot on May 19, 1998

It topped the US Billboard 200 album chart and garnered sales of more than 5 million copies in the first week.

Subsequently, the late rap artist Dark Man X released yet another studio called Flesh of My Flesh, Blood of My Blood on December 22, 1998

In the mean time, DMX launched his third studio album, … And Then There Was X on December 21, 1999 with Ruff Ryders and Def Jam Recording labels.

The track “Party Up (Up in Here)” from this album got the nomination for Best Rap Solo Performance by the Grammy Award in 2001.

The late American singer DMX dropped his fourth album entitled The Great Depression on October 23, 2001.

Likewise, Earl Simmons launched his fifth studio album called Grand Champ in collaboration with Def Jam Recordings.

Above all, the release of this album on September 16, 2003, created history as all of his five studios at the time debuted on the top spot of the US Billboard 200 albums chart.

Afterward, he switched to Columbia Records and released his sixth studio album, Year of the Dog… Again on August 1, 2006.

Meanwhile, following his sixth album release, Earl Simmons, aka DMX, took hiatus from music and started reading religious books to get some peace of mind.

After almost 6 years break, DMX (Dark Man X) released his seventh album entitled Undisputed.

To sum up, DMX’s posthumous studio album was released on May 28, 2021, entitled Exodus.
