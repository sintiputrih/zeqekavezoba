Professional Life

Shanthakumaran Sreesanth made his debut in domestic cricket in the 2002-03 season against Goa. In 2004, while playing against Himachal Pradesh in the Ranji Trophy, Sreesanth made a hat-trick. Following his excellent performance, he made it to India B for the Challenger Trophy in October 2005.

Similarly, In the Challenger Trophy, he was awarded as Man of the Series award. Consequently, he was promoted for selection in the National Team for the ODI series against Sri Lanka in 2005. In his six-year-long career, he took down 87 test wickets, 75 ODI wickets, and 7 T20 wickets for his country.

In 2008 he started playing for Kings XI Punjab till 2010 and from 2011 to 2013 he played for Kochi Tuskers Kerala. Later, he got drafted to Rajasthan Royals. He has played 44 matches in the IPL and took 40 wickets

In 2018, Sreesanth participated in Bigg Boss 12 as a celebrity contestant and became the first runner-up.

Controversy

On 25 April 2008, Kings XI Punjab defeated Mumbai Indians, Sreesanth scoffed at Harbhajan Singh captain of Kings XI Punjab. Following the events, Harbhajan Singh slapped Sreesanth right in front of all fans.

In the IPL season 2013, Sreesanth faced charges of spot-fixing along with Ajit Chandila and Ankeet Chavan. He also got banned for a lifetime but later changed it to a seven-year ban.

Come back after the ban

After a seven-year ban, he had previously talked about beginning “everything possible to get back on the field”. On 13 September 2020, the ban officially ended and he was selected for the Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy and Vijay Hazare Trophy in 2021 in the Kerala Team.
