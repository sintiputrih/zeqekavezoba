Dalmar Abuzeid Biography

Dalmar Abuzeid is an acclaimed Canadian actor. He became known for representing Danny Van Zandt in Degrassi: The Next Generation. In 2019, he earned acclaim for his performance in the Netflix series Anne with an E, winning an ACTRA Award and a Canadian Screen Award. With this character, he became the first Black actor to perform in the history of the Anne of Green Gables media franchise.

    Dalmar Abuzeid Actor

    Abuzeid came to the spotlight for representing the character Danny Van Zandt on the well-known Canadian TV series Degrassi: The Next Generation. He reprised this character in additional spin-off projects, including Degrassi Goes Hollywood and Degrassi Takes Manhattan.

    During this time, he also played the main character in the Teletoon series Majority Rules! and went on to star in guest roles in a variety of TV series, such as Murdoch Mysteries, Republic of Doyle, Hemlock Grove, and Dark Matter. In addition to acting, he has also been a host of TVOKids, and the Kids CBC series Snapshots.

    From 2018 – 2019, Abuzeid represented Sebastian ‘Bash’ Lacroix in the second and third seasons of Anne with an E; this marked the first time that a Black character has been featured since the franchise’s inception. He won the Canadian Screen Award for Best Guest Performance in a Drama Series at the 8th Canadian Screen Awards in 2020. He also won an ACTRA Award for his performance in Anne with an E.

    Dalmar Abuzeid Music

    Abuzeid played bass in the band SoundSpeed, a six-piece music group that also included four other Degrassi cast members Raymond Ablack (lead vocalist), Jamie Johnston (guitarist), Shane Kippel (drums), and Scott Paterson (keytar). They released a three-song EP in January 2010.
