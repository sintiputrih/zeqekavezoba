Xzibit Net Worth: Career &amp; Lifestyle

American rapper, singer, songwriter, actor, radio personality, and television presenter Xzibit’s net worth is a staggering $3 million.

Originally, Alvin Nathaniel Joiner, Xzibit was born on September 18, 1974, in Detroit, Michigan, USA.

In 1996, he began his musical career after releasing his debut studio album “At the Speed of Life.” 

The album was critically acclaimed and commercially successful, reaching number 74 on the Billboard 200 chart. After that, he started gaining fame in the West Coast hip-hop scene.

Similarly, “40 Dayz & 40 Nightz”, his second album, debuted at number 53 on the Billboard 200, attracting the attention of rap icon Dr. Dre.

In 2000, he released a platinum-certified album, “Restless,” with Dr. Dre as executive producer.

In addition, Xzibit rose to prominence as an actor and television host, thanks to his role in the hit television show “Empire” and reality television series “Pimp My Ride.”

Xzibit | Career

Xzibit began rapping in the underground hip-hop scene of Los Angeles. In 1995, he featured on albums by King Tee and Tha Alkaholics in the 1990s.

After releasing his first and second albums, Xzibit became a driving factor in hip-hop. He was able to sign a recording contract with Loud Records as a result of his success.

He appeared on tracks such as Snoop Dogg’s “Bitch Please,” Dr. Dre‘s “Lolo” and “Some L.A. Niggaz,” and Eminem‘s “What’s the Difference.“

In 2001, he co-starred in “The Wash.” Similarly, in 2001 and 2002, he co-starred with Eminem in “The Slim Shady Show” and “8 Mile”. 

In addition, he released two concert videos, “Xzibit: Restless Xposed” and “Xzibit: Restless Xposed,” in 2001, which documented the recording of his third studio album and different live performances.

Furthermore, Xzibit has released albums like “Man vs. Machine” (2002), “Weapons of Mass Destruction” (2004), and “Napalm” (2012).
