Pat Mahomes Sr: Bio

After getting hurt in his senior year of high school, Mahomes Sr started pitching the next year. He turned down scholarship offers to play football and basketball from Division I schools to play baseball at the University of Arkansas.

In 1988, the Minnesota Twins picked him in the sixth round of the MLB Draft. He chose to go pro right after high school. In 1992, he played his first game in the Major Leagues for the Twins. He did a great job for them for five years before being traded to the Boston Red Sox.

He played for the Red Sox for two years and was released from his contract. Until 2003, he played baseball for the New York Mets, the Texas Rangers, the Chicago Cubs, and the Pittsburgh Pirates.

Patrick Mahomes’s dad wanted him to stop playing football and start playing baseball. He thought that the NFL was too rough for his son, but Mahomes made the right choice and is now one of the best players in the league.

Mahomes thinks that working out, pitching, and playing basketball helped him become a better quarterback. As a senior in football, he threw for 4,619 yards and 50 touchdowns. He also ran for 948 yards and scored 15 touchdowns on the ground.

Mahomes was also a top prospect for the 2014 Major League Baseball draft, but he had already committed to play for Texas Tech, so it wasn’t likely that he would be picked high. In the 2014 MLB Draft, the Detroit Tigers picked him in the 37th round, but he did not sign a contract.

Patrick Mahomes: Social Justice Activism

Mahomes and other NFL players, like Saquon Barkley, DeAndre Hopkins, Odell Beckham Jr., Ezekiel Elliott, and Deshaun Watson, were in a Black Lives Matter video that went viral in July 2020. The video criticized police brutality.

In an August 2020 profile for GQ, he said, “I understand my platform.” “I’m in the middle of negotiating my next contract, and I hope to stay with the Kansas City Chiefs for a long time. But I thought this was important enough to say, so I did.

I couldn’t just sit back and worry about getting my next contract; I had to use my platform to help. It’s not always about money. Not about being famous. It comes down to doing the right thing.”

Soon after that, he joined NBA star LeBron James’s organization, More than a Vote, and helped with voter registration and efforts to keep people from voting. In a July tweet, he asked the more than 1.7 million people who follow him on Twitter to send him a screenshot of their confirmation of registration.

Before the 2020 general election, his 15 and the Mahomies Foundation split the cost of new voting machines in Kansas City with the Chiefs. Sports Illustrated named Mahomes a 2020 Sportsperson of the Year because of the work he has done to help people.
