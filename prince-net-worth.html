Prince | Net Worth, Will and Settlements & Estate

Net Worth

One of the greatest musicians of all time, Prince’s net worth was $200 million at the time of his death in 2016.

However, according to several sources, he was worth even more than that at the time.

With more than 100 million albums and 150 million record sales, Prince had amassed quite a fortune.

His second studio album Prince earned him $3 million in 1979.

Similarly, he released an album titled 1999 in 1982, which made Prince $4.35 million.

Prince’s biggest success yet came in the year 1984 with the release of his sixth studio album titled Purple Rain.

The album sold over 25 million copies worldwide and was certified a Diamond by RIAA. The most successful album of the artist made him $25 million.

Likewise, his 1985 album Around the World in a Day made $7 million, his 1986 album Parade made $2 million, his 1989 album made $11 million, and the compilation album The Very Best of Prince made $3.455 million for the singer.

In 2004 alone, Prince made $56.5 million from concerts and album and publishing sales.

Prince’s performance in 2008 Coachella earned him a paycheck of $5 million.

Tour Income

One of the greatest musicians was also one of the biggest concerts draws.

Prince had headlined several highest-grossing tours over the course of his career.

While the exact data of how many tours Prince had headlined is not available, sources claim that he did 30 identifiable tours over his 40 years career.

Out of that, his 2004 tour grossed over $87 million at the box office, making it the most successful tour of the year.

Will and Settlements

The estate settlement is perhaps the most hectic for the people involved in the procedure, especially when there is no proper will which is exactly what happened after Prince’s death.

The most important asset for musicians like Prince has to be their music rights and catalog, as it acts as a perpetual source of income via royalties.

In Prince’s case, the singer famously owned most of his work.

When he made his demise, there was no proper will; thus, the value of his entire estate was passed to six family members.

After Prince’s death, there was hysterical debate over his estate, which seemed pretty obvious as the case was hundreds of millions of dollars.

The administrator of the estate was Comerica Bank & Trust.

According to their estimation, Prince’s estate was worth around $82 million in total. This included all his real estate, name, likeness, and, most importantly, music rights.

However, contrasting the evaluation of CBT, Internal Revenue Service claimed that the estate was worth at least $163 million.

After a few trials and legal disputes, Comerica and IRS came to a common ground and valued Prince’s estate at $156 million in total.

With all said and done, the estate was evenly divided between three of his oldest family heirs and his music company Primary Wave.

Nevertheless, over the six-year period between Prince’s death and the final agreement, two of his six siblings died.

Interesting!

Disputed value of Prince’s Estate

After Prince’s death, the dispute between estate administrator Comerica Bank & Trust and IRS rose, with both claiming the different value of the estate.

The first and foremost dispute was over New Power Generation publishing. New Power Generation NPG was the backing band of Prince from 1990 to 2013.

Just so you know, NPG owns the Prince’s songwriting copyrights.

Over the NPG publishing, Comerica fixed the value of $21 million while IRS pegged $37 million.

Similarly, another dispute was over NPG Records, Prince’s record label.

Over this matter, Comerica pegged a $19.4 million valuation while the IRS pegged $46 million.

Likewise, Comerica valued “Writer’s Share” of the songwriting catalog a mere $11 million, while IRS fixed the value of a staggering $22 million.

Prince | Lifestyle & Death

Lifestyle

Prince’s net worth at the time of his death was a staggering $200 million.

One of the greatest names in the music industry Prince was a man with a super special personality and uncanny charm.

He lived his life to the fullest, or we believe he did.

Prince was romantically involved with several singers and models, including Madonna, Kim Basinger, Carmen Electra, and Susannah Melvoin.

He was an animal rights activist and followed a vegan diet for some part of his life.

However, he later described himself as a vegetarian.

Moreover, following more than two years of debate with bassist Larry Graham, Prince became a Jehovah’s Witness in 2001.

He never considered it the conversation but realization.

Prince was an avid guitar player, given his profession. He had one of the iconic and flamboyant custom guitar collections, with 121 guitars in total.

Death

Prince postponed two shows in Atlanta on April 7, 2016. He released the statement saying he was recovering from flu.

He performed the final show of his life on April 14 even though he was feeling unwell.

While getting back to Minneapolis the next morning, Prince did not show any sign of responsiveness on the airplane, and hence the private jet had to emergency land in Illinois.

The test later in the hospital revealed that he had an overdose of opioids.

Prince left the hospital against hospital advice.

However, on the morning of April 21, a 911 call came requesting an ambulance be sent to Paisley Park.

Prince was found without any sign of life, but still, the paramedics performed CPR, but nothing brought back the legend from the other side.

The 57 years old Prince left the world on April 21 because of an accidental overdose of the opioid Fentanyl.

Prince | Career

On April 27, 1978, Prince released his first album titled For You.

However, this did not perform well in the charts as well as in audiences.

He then released the self-titled album Prince in 1979. The album proved to be quite a bounce in his career, after which he never had to look back.

Prince’s sixth studio album Purple Rain released in 1984, sold over 13 million copies in the United States alone.

It spent 24 weeks straight at No. 1 on the Billboard charts.
