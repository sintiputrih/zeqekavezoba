Career of Johnny Walker

December 2013 saw the start of Johnny Walker’s professional mixed martial arts career, which has been marked by highs and lows. His early career in Brazil demonstrated his skill; over the course of four years, he recorded 10 wins—all by stoppages—and 3 losses. Walker, seeking fresh prospects, accepted a businessman’s offer to travel to Scotland, but left after only one month since he was not paid.

After relocating to England, he continued to win, amassing three more victories, and in August 2018, he finally caught the eye of Dana White’s Contender Series. Walker won his first fight on the Contender Series against Henrique da Silva to win a seat on the UFC roster. At UFC Fight Night 140 in November 2018, he made his promotional debut by eliminating Khalil Rountree Jr. in the opening round, earning his first Performance of the Night bonus.

With back-to-back knockout victories, Walker maintained his remarkable run and won back-to-back Performance of the Night prizes. Walker had losses, such the one against Corey Anderson in November 2019, but he also triumphed over Misha Cirkunov and Ryan Spann.

Walker’s fight with Spann was postponed due to COVID-19, but he won via first-round knockout. He encountered difficulties in the ensuing years, such as a chest ailment that forced him to withdraw from a match against Jimmy Crute.

Walker won via rear-naked choke submission in 2022 over Ion Cuțelaba, receiving his fourth Performance of the Night bonus. Interestingly, he defeated Paul Craig and Anthony Smith at the beginning of 2023, which set up a contentious no-contest with Magomed Ankalaev because of an illegal knee.

Walker faced Ankalaev once more in the rematch in January 2024, but he was eliminated in the second round. In the professional world of mixed martial arts, Johnny Walker’s career continues to be a monument to his tenacity and dynamic fighting style despite the ups and downs.
